All India Institute of Medical Sciences, Delhi (AIIMS Delhi) is a central autonomous institute located in Ansari Nagar, New Delhi. Established in 1956. In 2025, it was ranked 1st by Collegedunia, NIRF & many other ranking agencies in the medical & other categories.

  • The most popular courses offered at AIIMS Delhi are MBBS, MD & MS.
  • The Institute follows the Government of India reservation policy, including 5% reservation for PwBD candidates.
  • Admission to the MBBS course is based on NEET UG score, followed by MCC. MD & MS are based on the INI CET score, followed by counselling conducted by AIIMS Delhi.
  • The total fees for the MBBS course are INR 5,356, and for the MD/MS courses are INR 7,577.
  • In 2025, the last-round closing rank of  NEET UG for the General Category was 48, and INI CET was 4440.

AIIMS Delhi Admission 2026

Admission to all the courses at AIIMS Delhi is based on merit scores in the respective entrance examinations, followed by Counselling. The Institute follows the Government of India’s reservation policy, which includes 27% for OBC (Non-Creamy Layer), 15% for Scheduled Castes (SC), 7.5% for Scheduled Tribes (ST), and 10% for Economically Weaker Sections (EWS). A horizontal 5% reservation is provided for Persons with Benchmark Disability (PwBD).

AIIMS Delhi Campus

AIIMS Delhi Campus is located at Sri Aurobindo Marg, Ansari Nagar East, New Delhi. AIIMS offers a whole range of facilities to its students, from hostel accommodations to cafes within the campus. Here’s a detailed list of facility provisions at AIIMS Delhi:

  • Library: AIIMS has a very well-equipped and furnished library that contains more than 61,000 books, more than 53,000 journals, and more than 14,000 reports.
  • Hospital and Medical Facility: There are medical facilities available, such as a cancer hospital, a trauma center, specialty parent care with 464 beds, and a center for community medicine that offers services to more than 84,000 people and contains 50 beds.
  • Hostel Accommodation: There is hostel accommodation available for both boys and girls, built for the students to experience a homely feeling and cater to all the necessities of the students.
  • Cafeteria: The institute provides a cafe for students to eat healthy and hygienic food at an economical price within the campus.
  • Laboratories: Being the best medical research college in the country, the laboratories are well-equipped and contain all the important requirements as per the needs of the students.
  • IT Infrastructure: The AIIMS New Delhi campus provides world-class IT infrastructure. Computer labs are present for the students and faculty and are fully air-conditioned.

AIIMS Delhi FAQs

Ques. Is AIIMS Delhi the best college for MBBS?

Ans. AIIMS Delhi is regarded as the best medical institute in India for MBBS, known for its excellent learning experience, faculty, infrastructure, research opportunities, and clinical exposure. It has been ranked 1st in India in the medical category by all major ranking bodies, including NIRF, IIRF, India Today, and The Week. The institute has state-of-the-art infrastructure, well-equipped laboratories, libraries, and one of the most advanced medical facilities in India, providing students with ample resources for learning and research.

Ques. What is the minimum age required for MBBS admission at AIIMS Delhi?

Ans. Candidates applying for the MBBS program at AIIMS Delhi must be at least 17 years of age as of December 31 of the year of admission.

Ques. Can I get into AIIMS Delhi with NEET PG?

Ans. No, AIIMS Delhi conducts its own entrance exam, INI CET (Institute of National Importance Combined Entrance Test), for admission to its PG courses such as MD, MS, MDS, DM, and MCh. The institute doesn’t accept NEET PG for admission to the PG medical & dental courses.

Ques. Is AIIMS Delhi good for research?

Ans. AIIMS Delhi is highly regarded and known for its research activities and is considered one of the premier institutions for medical research in India. The institute boasts state-of-the-art research facilities, including well-equipped laboratories, advanced imaging centers, and specialized research units. The facilities at the institute support a wide range of research activities across various disciplines, from basic sciences to clinical research, which is far more advanced than any other medical institute in India. AIIMS Delhi is also known to provide a significant volume of research output, including scientific publications, patents, and innovations, which makes it undoubtedly one of the best research institutes.

Ques. How many seats are offered for MBBS at AIIMS Delhi?

Ans. AIIMS Delhi has a total of 132 MBBS seats, comprising 125 seats for Indian Nationals and 7 seats reserved for foreign nationals. The specific number of seats for each category is determined by the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) following the government's reservation policies.

Which is better AIIMS or JIPMER?

Kanika Singh

if we're comparing AIIMS Delhi vs JIPMER Pondicherry specifically, AIIMS Delhi wins — but not as decisively as people think.

AIIMS Delhi advantages: bigger hospital, more patient variety, better research funding, smaller batch (132 seats), stronger PG and super-specialty programs. JIPMER advantages: way more hands-on clinical exposure, internship is genuinely more intensive, calmer campus in Pondicherry, 150+ years old institution with incredible legacy — way older than AIIMS itself.

  • if you're from north India — AIIMS makes more sense since doctor-patient interaction is in Hindi
  • if you're from south India or want a focused clinical environment — JIPMER is genuinely excellent
  • JIPMER calls itself the AIIMS of the South and that's not really an exaggeration tbh

one thing most answers miss — this comparison only works for AIIMS Delhi and JIPMER Pondicherry. the new AIIMS campuses (Bhopal, Bibinagar, Raipur etc.) are much newer with limited infrastructure. JIPMER Pondicherry actually beats most of those hands down. preference order for most people: AIIMS Delhi > JIPMER Pondicherry > new AIIMS campuses.

What is the frequency of holidays and vacations in AIIMS Delhi?

Neha Awasthi

AIIMS Delhi holidays and vacations:

AIIMS Delhi follows a strict academic calendar given the nature of medical education:

  • MBBS students: very limited holidays compared to engineering/arts colleges. the clinical rotations are essentially year-round
  • Annual leave: approximately 30-40 days of formal holidays per year including national holidays, Diwali, Holi, summer break (usually 2-3 weeks)
  • During clinical postings (3rd-5th year MBBS): leaves are minimal. attendance is strictly monitored. taking unplanned leaves can affect your posting
  • Interns/residents: even more restricted — leave entitlement is formal (casual leaves, earned leaves) applied through department

compared to other colleges, AIIMS has a high workload and limited leisure time — this is the nature of medical training. the AIIMS academic calendar is demanding by design. students who chose AIIMS know this is part of the deal. the academic intensity is high but the training quality is also proportionally excellent.

Do students of AIIMS Delhi get opportunity to go to US UK during MBBS?

Yogesh Choudhary

yes, AIIMS Delhi students do get opportunities to go to US/UK during MBBS — but the reality is a bit more complicated than it sounds.

  • ICMR research grants: if you do solid research and get it approved by ICMR, the trip is fully funded. this is the best route. but you need actual research output, not just applying randomly
  • Self-funded electives: Oxford, Hopkins, Harvard all have elective programs open to AIIMS students. most students who go this route pay out of pocket. AIIMS administration actually discourages it since it means missing classes, but students manage it during vacations or by getting internship extended later
  • Formal exchange programs: AIIMS has MoUs with Harvard, Johns Hopkins, Yale, Oxford. spots are limited and selection is competitive — academic performance and research experience matter a lot

real talk — most AIIMS students are genuinely too busy during MBBS to go abroad. the ones who do are usually research-oriented or from very well-off families who can self-fund. if you're planning post-MBBS though, AIIMS is a great launchpad for USMLE (US medical licensing) and UK PLAB prep — the brand carries serious weight internationally.

How much marks should I score in the AIIMS entrance exam to secure an MBBS seat in AIIMS Delhi??

Amala Samuel

AIIMS Delhi no longer has a separate entrance exam — it joined the NEET system in 2020. so admission to AIIMS Delhi is through NEET UG marks only.

to get AIIMS Delhi (MBBS), you need to be among the top ~50-100 NEET rankers nationally (general category). in practice that means:

  • NEET score: 715-720 out of 720 (general category, recent trends)
  • NEET rank: approximately AIR 1-100 for general category
  • AIIMS Delhi closing rank for general typically falls between rank 1-50

this is the most competitive MBBS seat in India. getting even 700 in NEET would not guarantee AIIMS Delhi for general category.

for the newer AIIMS institutions (AIIMS Bhopal, Patna, Jodhpur, Rishikesh, Bhubaneswar, etc.):

  • NEET rank ~100-1000 for general category gets you into one of these
  • these are also excellent government medical colleges, world-class in terms of government funding

focus on maximizing your NEET score. 700+ keeps your options open for all AIIMS campuses.

Ph.D. Application Process 2025

  • After submitting your research proposal and paying the application fee, upload all the important documents
