WBNUJS, officially known as The West Bengal National University of Juridical Sciences, Kolkata, is a BCI-approved public law university located in Kolkata, West Bengal. Established in 1999, the university is recognized by the UGC and is one of the premier National Law Universities in India.

  • Ranking: Ranked 4th out of 40 colleges in India in the Law category by NIRF 2025.
  • Popular Course: BALLB {Hons.} (Total fees – INR 16.39 Lakhs) and L.L.M (Total fees – INR 1.79 Lakhs).
  • Entrance Exam: Admissions to the UG and PG law programmes are conducted through the CLAT, a national-level entrance examination.
  • Placement: NIRF 2025 reports a median package of INR 20 LPA (UG) and INR 10 LPA (PG), with 197 students placed.

WBNUJS admissions for 2026 are currently open through the CLAT 2026 centralized counselling process, and eligible candidates must register and participate to secure a seat.

WBNUJS Kolkata Important dates FAQs

Ques. How does WBNUJS release its admission dates?

Ans. WBNUJS announces its admission dates mainly through the CLAT schedule for UG and PG programmes. These dates are published online and follow the CLAT counselling process. Ph.D. admission dates are released separately by the university. Applicants should check official updates regularly.

Ques. Are WBNUJS UG and PG admission dates the same?

Ans. UG and PG admissions at WBNUJS generally follow the same CLAT counselling timeline. However, the number of rounds and seat allotment schedules may vary. Candidates should track programme-wise updates. Final admission depends on counselling outcomes.

WBNUJS Kolkata Courses and Fees FAQs

Ques. Are hostel and mess charges included in the course fees at WBNUJS?

Ans. No, hostel accommodation and mess charges are separate from tuition fees. Students must pay these fees directly to the university or hostel authorities. The charges cover lodging, food, and basic amenities. These costs are in addition to the academic fees for the course.

Ques. Does WBNUJS offer any installment facility for fee payment?

Ans. Yes, WBNUJS allows students to pay tuition fees in multiple installments. The university provides a schedule outlining deadlines for each installment. This helps students manage their finances more conveniently. Late payments may incur fines as per university rules.

WBNUJS Admission Process 2026

Admissions to WBNUJS, Kolkata are conducted for UG, PG, and Doctoral programmes through entrance exams, merit lists, and counselling. The process differs for UG/PG courses compared to research or certificate programmes.

1. Entrance Exam:

  • UG (B.A. LL.B. & B.Sc. LL.B.): Admission is based on CLAT 2026 scores.
  • PG (LL.M.): Admission is based on CLAT-PG 2026 scores.
  • Ph.D./LL.D: Admission is based on university notifications, research proposals, and interviews.

2. Application:

  • Candidates must complete the online application through the Consortium of NLUs portal for UG/PG programmes.
  • For Ph.D./LL.D., applications are submitted directly to WBNUJS as per departmental notification.

3. Merit List & Counselling (UG/PG):

  • Seats are allocated based on CLAT rank, category, and preferences submitted during counselling.
  • Multiple rounds of seat allotment are conducted.

4. Confirmation of Admission:

  • Candidates must pay the confirmation fee and select Freeze/Float options to secure the seat.
  • Final admission is confirmed after document verification at WBNUJS.

5. Special Programmes:

  • Certificate, M.Sc., and M.A. courses may have direct application and departmental selection, separate from CLAT.

WBNUJS Kolkata Admission FAQs

Ques. Are there any reserved seats at WBNUJS?

Ans. Yes, WBNUJS follows the Government of India and West Bengal reservation policy. Seats are reserved for SC, ST, OBC, PwD, and other eligible categories. Reservation is applied during merit list preparation and counseling. Applicants must provide valid documents to claim reservation benefits.

Ques. Can foreign nationals apply for admission at WBNUJS?

Ans. Yes, WBNUJS allows foreign nationals and NRI students to apply through a separate admission process. They may not need to appear for CLAT but must meet academic eligibility requirements. Fees for foreign students are higher and structured differently. Admissions are confirmed after evaluation of academic credentials and fee payment.

WBNUJS Kolkata Cutoff FAQs

Ques. Can WBNUJS cutoffs change during the admission process?

Ans. Yes, cutoffs may fluctuate during counseling rounds if seats remain unfilled or new applicants are considered. Each round publishes revised cutoffs based on remaining seats and category-specific merit. Candidates should follow official notifications to track changes. Meeting the cutoff is essential for eligibility, but admission depends on seat confirmation.

Ques. Are foreign or NRI students subject to WBNUJS cutoffs?

Ans. Foreign nationals and NRI candidates have a separate cutoff system or minimum eligibility criteria. They are generally not compared with CLAT merit lists but must meet academic requirements. Admissions for these candidates depend on evaluation of transcripts and fee payment. Cutoffs ensure that only qualified applicants are considered.

WBNUJS Kolkata Placement FAQs

Ques. Are internships mandatory for WBNUJS students?

Ans. Yes, internships are a mandatory part of WBNUJS programs, providing practical exposure to legal practice. Students undertake multiple internships during the course, including in law firms, courts, NGOs, and corporate legal departments. Internships often influence final placements and networking opportunities. The placement cell assists in securing high-quality internships.

Ques. Do WBNUJS placements include international opportunities?

Selection is highly competitive, based on merit, academic record, and interview performance. This gives students exposure to cross-border legal practices.

How is food in NLU Kolkata hostels?

SouvikStudent at NLU Kolkata

The food we get at the NLU Kolkata hostels is really good. The student body is in charge of the exceptionally successful university mess. Breakfast is laid between 8:00 am to 10:00 am, lunch is provided between 12:30 to 2:00 pm, and dinner is served between 8:00 pm to 10:00 pm. The hostel mess also serves snacks from 4:00 pm to 6:00 pm.

The mess menu is reviewed regularly by the Student Mess Committee, and they take suggestions from other students as well. We have options for both vegetarian and non-vegetarian students. Some of the most common food items that are served at the hostel mess are- milk, egg, fruits, upma, sandwiches, khichdi, rice, dal, roti, aloo masala, chicken samosa, biriyani, and gajar halwa among others.

Every month, the Mess Committee organises Cultural Dinners to honour the cuisines of various states and regions in India, in addition to their regular menu. Special dinners and breakfasts are held to honour other festive occasions as well.

There is a cafe on the university campus called "Little Sisters," which offers Chinese, South Indian, and North Indian food at extremely affordable prices. It also has its own selection of snacks, drinks, and eateries.

You must not worry about the food available at NLU Kolkata hostels, as the same is of good quality.

Why is the fee of NUJs so high compared to NLSIU and Nalsar?

Prakash Guptastudied at NALSAR University of Law, Hyderabad

There are no specific reasons as such for the high course fees of NUJS. NUJS Kolkata charges INR 11.49 lakhs for a BALLB (Hons) degree. But people usually don’t question it. Also, most students who attend NUJS can afford the fees. There are a good number of NRIs in each batch. Students from SC, ST categories have many scholarship options to fund their degrees. 

Also, NUJS offers great recruitment opportunities. Students from NUJS Kolkata received the highest CTC of INR 22 LPA during the 2021 placement drive. This is also one of the reasons why people don’t question the high course fee.

What kind of hardships do students suffer in top law colleges like NLSIU, Nalsar or NUJS?

Shaoni Sengupta,B.A.L.L.B. from West Bengal National University of Juridical Sciences, Kolkata (2022)

One of my friends studied at NUJS. According to her, hardships have varied reasons from academics to personal.

  • The Cases/readings are worth easily 500 pages every day, preparing summaries/case briefs, Tutorials and then projects, presentations, vivas, and the tiring final semesters all comprise the academic hardships.
  • Due to internships, you will miss your vacations. Internships are a temporary glimpse into what life will be like.
  • After getting the grind to excel and to get placed / PPOs, some students suffer from existential crises, emotional upheavals, urge for infinite sabbaticals, etc. 
  • The burden of studies and placements takes a toll on your personal life. 
  • Case briefs become a reason for discord rather than unity. 
  • The healthy competition suddenly gets replaced by a rat race. 

Lastly, all the things like hobbies, extra-curricular, etc. disappear. But even after all the hardships, it still is the best time of a student’s life at NLSIU.

What is the average placement package of the top law universities like VIZ NLSIU, NALSAR and NUJS?

Khushi SharmaStudying B. Com from Banasthali Vidyapeeth, Jaipur

In the top three law schools, the placement scenes are fantastic. 

In terms of placement, WBNUJS has been doing really well. In the past, it has received 100% placement (not inclusive of those who do not sit for placements). The graduating class of 2020 received offers for all 78 participants who participated in the recruitment process.

With 30 students taking Day Zero in NALSAR, a total of 28 offers were offered, with 17 being accepted.

Last year at NLSIU, 47 final-year students who opted for it were hired by 19 businesses. The average remuneration package was about Rs 18 lakhs. In 2020, 40 job offers were made during NLSIU's day zero recruitment, which set a record.

The average salary at each of the top three law schools is nearly identical. Domestic and international firms pay different amounts for traineeships, with the average foreign firm package ranging from INR 38 lakhs to 44 lakhs and the average domestic firm package ranging from INR 8 lakhs to 18 lakhs.

Also, it's not only about attending one of the top three law schools; possibilities abound and are available to students from all law schools. If you are dedicated and hardworking, you will find a place at the top.
